Frequently Asked Questions

What subjects does Smartmeister cover?

expand_more
Currently, we focus on core Math and Language skills (primarily German aka German following Primary School Curriculum - KMK Rahmenlehrplan). We will be also expanding into Science, Music/Arts and maybe Coding/Programming modules soon.

Is the curriculum aligned with school standards?

expand_more
Yes, our content is designed to align to the German primary school curriculum (Rahmenlehrplan/KMK Lehrplan), also complementing topics with additional depth, ensuring the skills learned are directly applicable to your child's school performance.

How long should a child use it daily?

expand_more
We recommend short, high-impact sessions of 10–15 minutes. The goal is not achieving hours of practice every day, but rather doing regular sessions multiple times a week. This prevents digital fatigue while maximizing long-term knowledge retention.

How safe is my child's data?

expand_more
Data safety is our top priority. 100% GDPR compliant, your child's data never leaves our secure servers or is sold to third parties. Data (exercise mistake, past mistakes, foundational weakness) that is exclusively used in correlation with AI does not require the transmission of any child-related profile-specific or identity-related information. Therefore your child's data is secure and structurally kept in its boundaries.

How do you use AI

expand_more
We only use AI in very few specific places to synthesise learning-related information (such as current and past mistakes, typical foundational gap, learning goal) in view of providing help and articulate aligned learning goals. AI is not used as a chatbot or text-interface, nor is it used as an interactive tool for children. We keep AI within very constrained boundaries, helping to make learning more effective.

Is is it less time consuming than primary school tutoring?

expand_more
Parents don't need to pick schedules and align their daily appointments to the availability of tutors. You don't need to ferry your loved one to the tutor and back. Also, kids have a better chance to learn at the exact time, when their concentration and attention works best. Learning does not wait for when the tutors are available.

Can parents manage multiple children in one account?

expand_more
We plan to develop an all-in-one family profile that allows separate profiles for up to three children, ensuring each child has their own personalized learning pathway and progress reports.

How does the waitlist work?

expand_more
Joining the waitlist secures your spot for Early Access. We roll out invites in batches to ensure every family gets a smooth onboarding experience.

How is this different from other learning apps?

expand_more
Unlike other linear elearning apps for primary school students, Smartmeister identifies *why* a child is stuck—not just *if* they are. We focus on deep learning and step by step progression suited to their individual learning style. One Observation shared by many parents: Comprehension aka complete understanding of subject matter increasingly proves to be a challenge for many children, whether from native German or bilingual families. Typical tutoring is not always a suitable solution to address these. Problems are not just rooted in foundational gaps, but also in language-related areas, whether due to lack of reading, too much screen time, distractions, reduced motivation or lack of daily at-home conversations. No child must be left behind.

Both founders of Smartmeister are also parents in bilingual families (German + other language). To that end the challenges of these children are taken at heart, because learning German curriculum subjects (in conjunction to plenty of special-use & educational explanation vocabulary) with lesser German proficiency is simply more challenging, whether that kid is a prodigy or a slow-paced moon-shot. As we discovered on our way, it is also native speaking German children struggling with task comprehension. For this exact reason Smartmeister provides smart tools that enable these children to learn without obstacles, boosting comprehension and learning performance.

Why adaptive learning often works better than Math or German tutoring

expand_more
Tutoring is not a one-fits-all tool to fix learning problems. It works good with some children, worse with others, also depending on availability, location and cost. Even one-on-one tutoring has its limits that are easy to overlook. Quality of a tutor varies enormously: finding someone able to accurately identify why a child struggles (not just where they make mistakes) takes experience and time. Good tutors are rarer, more expensive, and the vibes between tutor and child have to click, too. Then there's the schedule: a child who is tired, distracted (headspace is somewhere else) or simply not in the right mood on a Tuesday at 4pm gains a lot less from that session regardless of how good the tutor is. The optimal learning window doesn't wait for the appointment.

For subjects like maths a simple gap can quietly sabotage everything built on top of it. That is why subject- or topic-mapped insights and exercise difficulty adjustment matter. A tutor in two weekly sessions may spot that a child is struggling, but detecting the full picture of underlying gaps, and how they connect to foundational knowledge, is genuinely hard when session time is so limited.

The same applies to German language tutoring and comprehension: a child who doesn't understand the words "Unterschied" or "Begrenzung" in a maths isn't failing at maths. They're failing at vocabulary. The tutor might catch that specific issue in a session. Yet, identifying it as part of a broader pattern of language-driven confusion, and systematically addressing it, requires the kind of daily visibility that weekly tutoring simply cannot provide often. Adaptive learning works across every session, adjusting focus to where a child most needs it — consistently, and at whatever time of day actually works for the family.
